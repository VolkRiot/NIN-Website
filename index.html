Nine Inch Nails





Official Bio

Nine Inch Nails (abbreviated as NIN and stylized as NIИ) is an American industrial rock band founded in 1988 by Trent Reznor in Cleveland, Ohio.

The band was warmly received by Underground music audiences in its early years, releasing two highly influential albums during the 1990s that achieved widespread popularity – The Downward Spiral (1994) and The Fragile (1999) – and continued to release music at the turn of the millennium. Many of the band's songs became radio hits, and their entire catalog has reached record sales exceeding over 20 million copies worldwide, with 10 million sales certified in the United States alone.
